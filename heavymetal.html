_ HEAVY METAL _


Genre of rock music that includes a group of related styles that are intense, virtuosic, and powerful.
Driven by the aggressive sounds of the distorted electric guitar, heavy metal is arguably the most commercially successful genre of rock music.
Developed in the late 1960s and early 1970s, largely in the United Kingdom and the United States. Heavy metal’s popularity slumped during
the disco years at the end of the 1970s, but it became more successful than ever in the 1980s were fragmented into subgenres
(such as lite metal, death metal, and even Christian metal) during that decade.Heavy metal musicians and fans came under severe criticism in the 1980s.

Political and academic groups sprang up to blame the genre and its fans for causing everything from crime and violence to despondency and suicide.
But defenders of the music pointed out that there was no evidence that heavy metal’s exploration of madness and horror caused,
rather than articulated, these social ills.
Since the mid-1990s until now, popular styles have expanded the definition of the genre. These include groove metal and nu metal,
the latter of which often incorporates elements of grunge and hip hop.

Bands: Metallica, Iron Maiden, Black Sabbath, Judas Priest, Motorhead, Slayer.
